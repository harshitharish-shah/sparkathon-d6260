Part I — User Guide

Overview

Promotion Intelligence answers one question for whoever is looking at it: who needs my attention, and are they actually ready? It replaces a gut-feel promotion conversation with a scored, evidenced, auditable one — without removing the human decision at the end.

The platform has two workspaces today. Home is the daily triage screen — counts and short worklists, scoped to whoever is logged in. Promotion is where a candidate is actually evaluated, discussed, and moved through approval — from "looks ready" to "promoted, on the record."

Every number on screen is tagged with where it came from. A green Live chip means it's real data today; an amber Partial or grey Projected chip means it needs an integration that isn't connected yet. Nothing is presented as certain when it isn't — see Reading the data-source chips.

Why no manual filter on Home: the scope is fixed at login rather than chosen on screen, so nobody can accidentally see outside their remit, and the same screen means the same thing for every viewer of that role.

The role switcher in the top bar is a prototype-only device. It lets you preview all four perspectives from one browser session. A real user signs in once and always sees the same role — there is no in-app way to switch identities.

Role-by-role reference

What each persona can see and do, screen by screen. Every action button in the product is role-aware — this is the map of which button appears for whom, and why.

ROLE · AGENT — signs in as themselves

Agent

Access: the Employee Workspace only, permanently open to their own record. No Home, no Promotion Dashboard, no Approval Workflow — an agent cannot see anyone else's data or act on anyone else's case.

  • Overview tab — their own readiness, decision, and eligibility checklist; they can set or update their career aspiration.
  • Evidence Locker — see exactly which sources back their own score, and whether each is live or pending an integration.
  • AI Insights — ask the assistant directly, e.g. "why aren't I ready yet" or "what should I focus on."
  • Career Journey — see the ladder ahead of them and which courses close their current skill gaps.

What an Agent cannot do: approve, recommend, or comment on their own case — the Approval Workflow, Compare Candidates, and Consistency Review screens don't exist for this role. Manager notes are read-only to them; only a supervisor can add one.

ROLE · SUPERVISOR — signs in as Rachel Torres

Supervisor

Access: Home, Promotion, and the Employee Workspace for anyone on their own team. No site or cross-team filter — a Supervisor has exactly one team, so there's nothing to filter by.

  • Home — Awaiting Review, Promotions Pending, High-Risk, Coaching Pending, Development Actions, and Career Recommendations, all scoped to their team. No Bench Strength tile — that's an Ops Mgr / HR view.
  • Promotion Dashboard — the light filter bar (readiness / decision / status only); the Supervisor column is hidden from the table since every row is already their own report. Row action: Recommend, shown only once a candidate is READY.
  • Approval Workflow — owns the first stage, Supervisor Review. Can Approve & advance or Delay a case at that stage, and is the one who provides evidence whenever Operations or HR requests more of it further up the chain.
  • Quick Actions — Review Promotions · View Employee · Compare Employees · Generate Promotion Packet.
ROLE · OPERATIONS MANAGER — signs in as Daniel Wu

Operations Manager

Access: Home, Promotion, and the Employee Workspace for anyone under the supervisors they oversee.

  • Home — the same tile set as Supervisor, scoped to "my teams and sites," plus the Bench Strength tile — a coverage view Supervisors don't get, since it only means something across multiple teams.
  • Promotion Dashboard — a fuller filter bar (supervisor/team, site, readiness, cycle); the Supervisor column is shown so cases can be compared across teams. Row action: Compare, which jumps straight into Compare Candidates.
  • Approval Workflow — owns Operations Review, the second stage for any target above the most junior tier. Can request evidence from the Supervisor, approve & advance, send back, or delay.
  • Consistency Review — runs the calibration discussion across their supervisors, flagging over-recommended candidates and missed talent before cases move further.
  • Quick Actions — Review Promotions · Compare Employees · Open Consistency Review · Generate Promotion Packet.
ROLE · HUMAN RESOURCES — signs in as Karen Blake

Human Resources

Access: the broadest scope — Home, Promotion, and the Employee Workspace for the entire business unit.

  • Home — the only role without a Coaching Pending tile (coaching is a supervisor-level concern); carries Bench Strength like Operations Manager, scoped to the whole business unit.
  • Promotion Dashboard — the full filter bar: business unit, department, site, supervisor, readiness, cycle. Row action: Packet, opening the Promotion Packet directly — HR's job is the governance record, not the initial call.
  • Approval Workflow — owns both HR Governance and, in this prototype, Leadership Approval (mapped to HR until a dedicated Leadership/Director persona exists). Can request evidence, approve & advance, send back, or delay at either stage — and the final approval step additionally checks that an open, funded requisition exists for the target role before it can be signed off.
  • Consistency Review — same calibration tooling as Operations Manager, at business-unit scope.
  • Quick Actions — Review Promotions · Open Consistency Review · Approvals & Governance · Workforce Analytics · Generate Promotion Packet.

Prototype quirk worth knowing: Leadership Approval is currently routed to HR because no separate Leadership/Director role exists yet in the app. If that persona is added later, this stage should move to them.

Employee Workspace

The full record for one person — readiness, evidence, competencies, and career history in one place. It's opened two ways: a Supervisor/Ops Mgr/HR clicks into it from Home, the Promotion Dashboard, Compare, or a search result to review a candidate; and it's the screen an Agent lands on when they log in — their role has no access to Home or Promotion, only their own Employee Workspace.

Same screen, two audiences: a manager opens someone else's record to decide; an agent opens their own record to understand where they stand. The tabs, charts, and evidence are identical either way — only whose name is on it changes.

The seven tabs

EMPLOYEE WORKSPACE → OVERVIEW

Overview

The landing tab. Employee information (role, career goal, manager, tenure) alongside the Promotion Readiness donut and decision pill, a Readiness Summary breaking out Career Fit / Leadership / Learning Progress / AI Confidence as bars, an Eligibility checklist (tenure, certification, compliance, has-evaluations), and a Career Aspiration & Manager Notes panel where a manager can log a free-text note. Closes with a one-line AI summary.

Aspirations and manager notes are write-back, not measured — the agent's stated career goal and a manager's note are stored as context; they don't feed the readiness score.

EMPLOYEE WORKSPACE → PERFORMANCE

Performance

Trend lines for Quality & CSAT and Productivity & Attendance over the last six periods, plus three callouts: Coaching Impact (pre- vs. post-coaching point change), Compliance status (Clear / Flagged — a flag is a hard block on promotion), and Attrition Risk (Low / Medium / High).

EMPLOYEE WORKSPACE → COMPETENCIES

Competencies

An eight-axis radar chart — Communication, Coaching, Leadership, Customer Focus, Ownership, Problem Solving, Collaboration, Learning Agility — each with its own value, plus a Skill Gaps & Recommended Focus card listing the three lowest-scoring axes and a suggested development path. Four of the eight axes read from live evaluation/behavioral data today; the rest are placeholders until a competency source is connected.

EMPLOYEE WORKSPACE → EVIDENCE LOCKER

Evidence Locker

The literal answer to "what is this score based on." Eight evidence sources are listed — Quality Evaluations, Coaching Sessions, Interaction Analytics, Customer Feedback, Recognition, Certifications, Performance Reviews, Manager Notes — each tagged available or pending integration. Above the list, an optional requested evidence card tracks a specific ask (e.g. HR requesting a document) through pending → provided. Below it, four tiles summarize Evidence Completeness, Confidence, Evidence Trust, and Audited Evaluations.

Why this tab exists: "every recommendation must trace to evidence" is the platform's core promise — this is where a skeptical reviewer (or the agent themselves) goes to check that promise, source by source.

EMPLOYEE WORKSPACE → AI INSIGHTS

AI Insights

Four generated call-outs — Promotion (the readiness verdict), Career (best-fit next role and its fit %), Leadership (the leadership index and what it implies), and Development (which skill gaps to close) — next to a Reasoning & Contributing Factors panel showing the top five competency scores as bars and the overall AI Confidence. A "Ask the assistant why →" link opens the same AI Assistant described below, already primed on this person.

EMPLOYEE WORKSPACE → CAREER JOURNEY

Career Journey

A Career Explorer strip showing the ladder from the current role forward with today's position marked, a Career Readiness donut, a Recommended Roles list ranked by fit %, and a Learning Roadmap mapping each skill gap to a course. The ladder shown here is illustrative until role ontology and competency requirements are configured (see Career Fit).

EMPLOYEE WORKSPACE → TIMELINE

Timeline

A chronological log — joining, onboarding, certifications earned, coaching sessions, recognition, evaluations — read top-to-bottom as the person's story rather than a snapshot. Coaching and evaluation entries are live; joining, training, certification, and award entries need an HRIS/LMS connection.

Ask AI (assistant)

A slide-over chat, opened from the Ask AI button in the top bar (or "Ask the assistant why" inside AI Insights), that answers plain-language questions grounded in the same evidence shown on screen — "Why isn't Marcus Johnson promotion-ready?", "Who is ready for Team Lead?", "Compare Sarah Mitchell and James Okafor." It's explanatory, not a separate source of truth: every answer traces back to the readiness, evidence, and competency data already on the candidate's own screens.

PROMOTION → APPROVAL WORKFLOW

Approval Workflow

Only candidates marked READY enter this chain. The chain's length depends on how senior the target role is — a rung-1 move (e.g. Agent → Senior Agent) needs fewer sign-offs than a move into management.

Target seniorityChain
Tier 1 — individual contributorSupervisor Review → HR Governance → Approved
Tier 2 — lead / analyst / specialistSupervisor Review → Operations Review → HR Governance → Approved
Tier 3 — manager / directorSupervisor Review → Operations Review → HR Governance → Leadership Approval → Approved

Each stage records who acted, when, and why — the trail that makes the eventual decision defensible in an audit.

PROMOTION → COMPARE CANDIDATES

Compare Candidates

Launched as an action, not a tab in its own right. By position auto-loads every READY candidate for a target role across supervisors; custom selection lets you add any agents you choose. Scores that already roll into Readiness (Quality, Behavioral, etc.) are omitted from the comparison so you're not re-reading the same number twice — Confidence is shown alongside so thin data isn't over-read.

PROMOTION → CONSISTENCY REVIEW

Consistency Review (Calibration & Fairness)

Each cycle, the panel — Operations Manager, HR, and supervisors — reviews flagged mismatches between a supervisor's recommendations and the scored evidence, and discusses the outliers: agents who look over-recommended versus missed talent the data suggests but no supervisor has flagged. This screen is the diagnostic; the actual selection happens in Compare Candidates, and sign-off happens in Approval Workflow.

PROMOTION → PROMOTION PACKET

Promotion Packet

The decision document for one candidate — readiness, evidence, eligibility, and recommendation assembled into a single printable record. This is what a compliance failure or an "unverified gate" gets written onto: e.g. "blocked by compliance gate" rather than a silent NOT YET.

Typical workflows

Supervisor — weekly review

  1. Open Home → check Awaiting Review and Coaching Pending.
  2. Click through to Promotion Dashboard, filtered to your team.
  3. For a READY candidate, use Recommend directly from the row, or open the Packet to review the full evidence first.
  4. Submitting a recommendation puts the case into Approval Workflow at Supervisor Review.

Operations Manager — calibration cycle

  1. Home → Bench Strength, to see where coverage gaps are before the cycle starts.
  2. Consistency Review → resolve flagged mismatches across supervisors.
  3. Compare Candidates "by position" → shortlist across teams for a given open role.
  4. Advance shortlisted cases through Approval Workflow at Operations Review.

HR — governance & sign-off

  1. Home → Awaiting Review shows cases that have reached the HR governance step.
  2. Open the Promotion Packet for each — this is the audit-ready record.
  3. Check Eligibility explicitly: a strong readiness score never overrides a compliance or tenure block.
  4. Approve in Approval Workflow; the case is stamped with the current promotion cycle.

Part II — Technical Guide

Readiness is a two-level weighted roll-up sitting on top of raw source data, with its own override and its own separate policy checks. Six layers, L0 through L6, follow the data end to end: external systems → per-agent dimensions → weighted umbrellas → the Readiness composite → the parallel checks that produce a Decision → the policy/fit axes checked separately from it. Weights 1–5 & 7–9 sum to the 66-point core-model total actually enabled today (22+18+14+8+4). Umbrella 10 activates only for supervisory targets, at which point weights renormalize.

Umbrella 1 — Quality (Evaluation) Live · weight 22

The evaluator-judged assessment of core-role performance. It's the heaviest umbrella because it's the strongest single proof of mastery — and capped at 22 so no single umbrella can decide a promotion alone.

Quality Score = average(ScorePercent) across qualifying evaluations ScorePercent = CurrentScoredPoints ÷ CurrentAvailablePoints × 100 Consistency = 100 − stdDev(ScorePercent) × 5 // steadier is safer Trajectory = regression slope of ScorePercent over time // signal only, weight 0 Umbrella score = (16 × QualityScore + 6 × Consistency) ÷ (16 + 6)
Six evaluations: 88, 92, 85, 90, 87, 91 → Quality Score 88.8 · stdDev ≈2.5 → Consistency 87.5 → Umbrella = (16×88.8 + 6×87.5) ÷ 22 = 88.4

Excluded from the average: in-progress evaluations, self-evaluations (they feed Self-Awareness instead), and calibration evaluations. Fewer than 3 evaluations drops Consistency; fewer than 4 leaves Trajectory unset; 5+ gives full confidence weight.

Umbrella 2 — Conversation & Behavior Live · weight 18

Scored on effectively every interaction rather than a handful of sampled evaluations — broad, largely unbiased coverage of how the agent actually works with customers.

Behavioral = average(8 Enlighten behavior indices) × 10 // Inappropriate Action excluded Sentiment = (average(SentimentScore) + 2) ÷ 4 × 100 // −2..+2 range → 0..100 Volume/Coverage = min(100, interactionCount) Umbrella score = (12 × Behavioral + 4 × Sentiment + 2 × Volume) ÷ (12 + 4 + 2)
8 indices avg 8.0 → Behavioral 80.0 · avg sentiment 0.6 → Sentiment 65.0 · 140 interactions → Volume 100 → Umbrella = 78.9

Umbrella 3 — Coaching & Growth Live · weight 14

The "will they grow" signal — whether the agent responds to coaching and knows their own gaps — as distinct from "how good are they today."

Coaching Impact = min(100, max(0, 50 + delta × 5)) delta = avg(ScorePercent after 1st coaching) − avg(ScorePercent before) Self-Awareness = 100 − avg(|selfScore% − supervisorScore%|) × 3 Umbrella score = (10 × CoachingImpact + 4 × SelfAwareness) ÷ (10 + 4)
82 → 86 after coaching: delta +4 → Coaching Impact 70.0 · self 90% vs supervisor 85%: gap 5 → Self-Awareness 85.0 → Umbrella = 74.3

Weighting & redistribution

Every umbrella and every dimension inside it carries a weight and a required: true/false flag — nothing about the model is hardcoded.

umbrellaScore = Σ(dimWeight × dimValue) ÷ Σ(dimWeight of dimensions present) readinessScore = Σ(umbrellaWeight × umbrellaScore) ÷ Σ(umbrellaWeight of umbrellas present)

Dividing by the weight that's actually present — not a fixed total — is what keeps the result on a genuine 0–100 scale no matter how many umbrellas a given customer has connected. That's what makes an agent with 4 live umbrellas comparable to one with 8.

What happens when something is missing

CaseMeaningBehavior
Expected absenceThe umbrella isn't enabled for this customer — they don't own that productRedistribute silently; screen notes "not connected"
Unexpected absenceEnabled but no data — a broken feed, or none for this agentFlag it, reduce Confidence, block READY if required
Required-missingA required umbrella (Quality, by default) has no data at allINSUFFICIENT DATA — no number is produced

Why this matters: silent redistribution of an enabled-but-broken feed would let a dead integration quietly inflate everyone's readiness. Flagging keeps the score honest — a gap you didn't buy is fine; a gap you didn't expect must surface.

What's configurable

  • Enable/disable an umbrella — weights renormalize automatically
  • The required flag, per umbrella and per dimension
  • The weight, per umbrella and per dimension
  • Per-target-role weight profiles — a Team Lead track can weight Coaching higher; a QA track can weight Quality/Compliance higher
  • Thresholds — READY 85, DEVELOPING 70, evidence floor 60, INSUFFICIENT floor 20

Decision rule

The label everyone reads the same way. Decision ≠ approval — it's "ready on the evidence," evaluated top-down, first match wins.

OrderConditionResult
1A required umbrella has no data, or Evidence Completeness < 20INSUFFICIENT DATA
2Compliance / Conduct Gate = FAILNOT YET
3Readiness ≥ 85 and Evidence Completeness ≥ 60READY
4Readiness ≥ 70DEVELOPING
5Readiness > 0 (none of the above)NOT YET
Evidence Completeness = round(100 × coverageFactor × depthFactor) coverageFactor = Σ[weight(umbrella) × fillRatio(umbrella)] ÷ Σ weight(enabled umbrellas) fillRatio = Σ dimWeight(dimensions with data) ÷ Σ dimWeight(all its dimensions) depthFactor = min(1, evaluationCount ÷ 5)

Coverage and depth are multiplied, not added — broad coverage on a single evaluation is still weak, and many evaluations confined to one umbrella is still narrow. Either factor being low pulls the whole score down.

Enabled: Quality 22(required) + Conversation 18 + Coaching 14 + Tenure 4 + Learning 8 = 66. All present except Learning: coverageFactor = 58÷66 = 0.88. 6 evaluations → depthFactor 1.0 → Evidence 88 → eligible for READY. If Quality itself were missing: INSUFFICIENT DATA, regardless of everything else.

Two agents can share Readiness 82 without being equally trustworthy — Confidence separates a thin-data 82 from a deep, recent, broad 82. It shares its coverageFactor definition with Evidence Completeness — one source of truth.

Trajectory

Direction predicts the future: a rising 78 can beat a falling 88. Target state is a regression over monthly readiness snapshots (every enabled umbrella); until snapshots accumulate, it runs as a clearly-labelled quality-only proxy.

slope = Σ((xᵢ−x̄)(yᵢ−ȳ)) ÷ Σ((xᵢ−x̄)²) // least-squares, x = month, y = readiness label = rising if slope ≥ +1.5/mo · falling if slope ≤ −1.5/mo · else flat
Five snapshots 74, 77, 80, 82, 85 → slope = 27.0 ÷ 10 = 2.7/month → RISING (≥ 1.5)

Fewer than 4 snapshots reads "insufficient history" rather than guessing a direction.

Eligibility gate

A single pass/fail prerequisite check — "is this agent allowed to be promoted per policy" — kept deliberately separate from Readiness ("are they good enough") and Confidence ("how sure are we"). A gate can't be out-voted by a strong score elsewhere.

CheckPass conditionStatus
Minimum TenuretenureMonths ≥ minTenure (default 6)Partial — proxy
Mandatory CertificationsAll role-required certifications currentConnect to enable
ComplianceNo critical/automatic-fail item in the review windowLive (evaluation)
Conduct FlagsZero open disciplinary casesConnect to enable

Eligible only if all four pass. On a compliance/conduct FAIL: a hard block, Decision forced to NOT YET. On a tenure/certification FAIL: shown as Ineligible — Readiness still stands, remedy is "wait" or "certify," not "coach." An unverifiable check (source not connected) is flagged, never silently passed.

Career Fit

A different question from Readiness: not "are they ready" but "is this the right role, and which one fits best." Computed as a competency gap against a role profile, then ranked across every candidate role.

gap[c] = max(0, roleTarget[c] − agentScore[c]) // only shortfalls count CareerFit = 100 − (Σ compWeight[c] × gap[c] ÷ Σ compWeight[c])
Team Lead profile (weights sum to 9): Communication 88 vs target 85 → gap 0 · Coaching 72 vs 85 → gap 13×2 · Leadership 60 vs 85 → gap 25×3 · Ownership 80 vs 85 → gap 5×2 · Problem Solving 78 vs 80 → gap 2×1
weighted = (0+26+75+10+2)÷9 = 12.6 → CareerFit = 100 − 12.6 = 87.4

Blocked on two things today: role profiles (required level + weight per competency, per role) aren't configured yet, and some competencies — Leadership, Collaboration, Systems, Analytical Thinking, Time Management, Adaptability — have no product source at all. Until then the column shows — · set up, or a dimmed partial score with the missing competencies named.

Design principle throughout: weighted average + renormalize keeps every score on the same 0–100 scale regardless of coverage; a mandatory floor means a decision is never made on missing core evidence; and every gap is flagged rather than hidden — a broken feed can't silently inflate a score.
